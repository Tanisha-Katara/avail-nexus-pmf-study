Context: What is Avail Nexus?

Understanding the product and market opportunity before diving into validation research.

Avail Nexus: Intent-Based Cross-Chain Orchestration

Avail Nexus is an intent-solving infrastructure that enables seamless token swaps across any network. Instead of users manually bridging, swapping, and managing complex multi-chain transactions, they simply declare their intent: "I want to convert X token on Network A to Y token on Network B."

Any-to-Any Swaps

Swap any token on any network to any other token on any other network

Intent Solver Framework

Declare desired outcomes; backend optimizes routing, bridges, and DEX aggregation

Non-Custodial

Integrates with existing custody infrastructure (Fireblocks, Safe, BitGo)

Optimized Execution

ERC-7683 standard ensures competitive solver marketplace for best pricing

Core Value Proposition: Abstract multi-chain complexity away from users and developers, enabling seamless cross-chain operations without building custom integrations for every network.

Research Methodology

A systematic, hypothesis-driven approach to discovering product-market fit through customer discovery and rapid validation.

1

Hypothesis Formation

Identified core problem: Organizations managing assets across multiple blockchain networks face significant operational overhead requiring consolidation infrastructure.

Initial Segments: Validators, OTC Desks, DeFi Traders

2

Parallel Testing

Conducted 5-7 interviews per segment concurrently to maximize learning velocity. Diversified targets by size, geography, and operational specialty.

Total Interviews: 12 completed (5 validators, 7 OTC/market makers)

3

Pattern Recognition

Mapped actual workflows vs. assumed workflows. Documented infrastructure patterns, custody preferences, and existing workarounds.

Key Framework: Jobs-to-be-Done analysis

4

Segment Bucketing

Created 3-tiered prioritization: (1) Target now - validated pain + willingness, (2) Test later - interested but have workarounds, (3) Deprioritize - no pain or already solved.

5

Rapid Pivot Decisions

Pivot criterion: If <50% of interviews validate core hypothesis, pivot to adjacent segment or reframe problem.

Result: Validated validators segment; invalidated OTC institutional desks

6

Market Sizing Validation

Secondary research to validate TAM for confirmed segments. Analyzed on-chain data, industry reports, and competitive landscape.

Core Research Frameworks Applied

Jobs-to-Be-Done

What are customers "hiring" current solutions to do? Why are those solutions insufficient?

Pain/Frequency Matrix

High pain + high frequency = immediate target. Low on either dimension = deprioritize.

Workflow Mapping

Document actual infrastructure (custody, networks, tools) vs. assumed infrastructure.

Competitive Alternative Analysis

What do they use today? Why is it insufficient? Would they switch?

Quote Validation

Capture direct customer language about solution value to validate problem-solution fit.

Progressive GTM

Early adopters → power users → mainstream institutions. Don't start with the hardest segment.

PMF Validation Signals

Clear criteria for determining product-market fit based on customer interviews and behavioral patterns.

✓ Positive PMF Signals (Validators)

  • Direct quote validation: "You become a 'validator treasury OS'" — customer articulates solution value in their own language
  • Quantified pain: 30-70 networks, hundreds of payout streams, 10+ custody systems — measurable complexity
  • Current inadequate workarounds: Using trader-focused tools (Li.fi, 1inch) that don't meet validator needs
  • Willingness to adopt: Interest in non-custodial routing middleware that integrates with existing custody
  • Emerging catalyst: DVT adoption increasing consolidation frequency from monthly to daily

✗ Negative PMF Signals (OTC Desks)

  • Problem reframing: "Multi-chain fragmentation isn't a universal OTC problem" — hypothesis rejected by majority
  • Low frequency: 95% of trades use standardized settlements — pain point doesn't occur often enough
  • Existing solutions: "Already have existing teams or rails" — adequate alternatives in place
  • Behavior contradiction: "Clients stick to one non-standard network" — actual behavior differs from hypothesis
  • Smallest bucket: "Don't have such client needs" — problem doesn't exist for target segment

Key Strategic Insights

Critical learnings from the PMF research process that inform product strategy and go-to-market decisions.

Insight 1: Infrastructure Patterns Matter

  • EVM chains: Safe (Gnosis) dominates validator treasuries
  • Cosmos SDK: Multisig withdraw addresses for commission sweeps
  • Solana: Squads multisig for validator operations
  • MPC custodians: Fireblocks/BitGo/Coinbase Prime for policy controls

Implication: Must integrate with existing custody infrastructure, not replace it. "Non-custodial routing middleware" positioning resonates.

Insight 2: DVT as Market Catalyst

  • Major exchange publicly moved to full DVT
  • Rewards fragmented across more operators
  • Increases consolidation frequency: monthly → daily
  • New workflow: "Sweep 40 micro-payouts daily from DVT clusters"

Implication: DVT adoption timing creates urgency for consolidation solutions. Early mover advantage available.

Insight 3: Unlisted Token Gap

  • Validators receive rewards in emerging network tokens
  • Current tools (DEX aggregators) built for well-listed assets
  • No adequate solution for converting obscure tokens to USDC/USDT
  • Workaround: Manual multi-step process across multiple DEXs

Implication: Niche but high-value use case. Validators willing to pay premium for this capability.

Insight 4: Network Concentration in OTC

  • Solana dominates as primary non-mainnet choice globally
  • Tron dominates APAC on/off-ramping (51% of USDT circulation)
  • Clients stick to one network based on investment thesis
  • 95% of institutional OTC trades use standardized settlements

Implication: Institutional OTC desks aren't the right entry point. Their end clients (DeFi traders, validators) are better targets.

Insight 5: Custody Split

  • Self-custody: Fireblocks, Safe, Squads (crypto-native orgs)
  • Third-party custody: Coinbase Prime, BitGo (regulated funds)
  • Regulatory constraint: Fund entities often prohibited from self-custody
  • Integration complexity: 10+ custody/treasury systems per validator

Implication: Must support both self-custody and third-party custody workflows. Integration flexibility is key differentiator.

Insight 6: Progressive Customer Acquisition

  • Validators: Technical sophistication, validated pain (target now)
  • DeFi power users: High-frequency need, early adopters (target next)
  • Institutional clients: Move slowly, need proven solutions (target later)
  • Traditional finance: Regulatory hurdles, long sales cycles (future)

Implication: Don't start with the hardest segment. Build credibility with early adopters before moving upstream to institutions.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Sequenced approach based on PMF validation signals and progressive customer acquisition principles.

Phase 1: Validators (Early Adopters)

Target: Validators managing unlisted tokens + DVT operators

Why: Validated pain, no adequate solutions, technical sophistication, willingness to adopt

Entry Point: "Validator treasury OS" positioning

Key Value Props: Unlisted token conversion, DVT micro-payment consolidation, custody integration

Success Metric: 10-15 validator customers within 6 months

Phase 2: DeFi Power Users

Target: Traders accumulating assets across networks via liquidity provision

Why: Genuine demand for consolidation, high-frequency use case, natural early adopters

Entry Point: Cross-chain yield optimization

Key Value Props: Simplified multi-chain position management, optimized routing, gas efficiency

Success Metric: $10M+ monthly volume from DeFi users

Phase 3: Crypto-Native Institutions

Target: Crypto-native funds, DAOs, protocol treasuries

Why: Proven solution with validator and DeFi user traction, move faster than traditional institutions

Entry Point: Treasury-as-a-service

Key Value Props: Programmatic treasury management, policy controls, audit trails

Success Metric: 5+ institutional customers, $100M+ AUM managed

Phase 4: Progressive Institutional Acquisition

Target: Traditional institutions exploring crypto treasury operations

Why: "They will move slowly, which is why we start with crypto-native institutions first"

Entry Point: White-label settlement infrastructure

Key Value Props: Regulatory compliance, wholesale CBDC readiness, proven track record

Success Metric: Strategic partnerships with traditional financial institutions
